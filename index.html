RAID Protection Explained

RAID Type Purpose
RAID 0 Combines drives for speed but provides no protection.
RAID 1 Mirrors data between drives for redundancy.
RAID 5 Balances capacity, speed, and protection.
RAID 6 Protects against multiple drive failures.

📀 Long-Term Storage Available Today

These technologies are currently available and used for real-world preservation and backup.

📀 M-DISC

A specialized optical disc designed for archival storage by using a more durable recording layer than traditional optical media.

Claimed lifespan:
100+ years

📼 LTO Magnetic Tape

A professional archival format widely used by companies, research institutions, and data centers.

Typical lifespan:
20-30 years

💿 Archival Blu-ray

Optical media designed for cold storage and long-term data preservation.

Potential lifespan:
50+ years

🗄 Enterprise HDD Archives

Large-scale hard drive systems used for backup and archive storage.

Typical lifespan:
5-10 years

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the fastest consumer storage?

High-end NVMe SSDs are currently among the fastest consumer storage technologies, reaching several thousand MB/s.

What storage lasts the longest?

Current archival technologies such as M-DISC and LTO tape are designed for long-term preservation. Experimental technologies such as DNA and quartz crystal storage may last much longer but are still being researched.

Is NAS better than cloud storage?

NAS provides control over your own hardware while cloud storage offers easier remote access and off-site backup.

How much data can future storage hold?

Technologies such as DNA, atomic, molecular, and crystal storage could greatly increase storage density compared with current drives.
