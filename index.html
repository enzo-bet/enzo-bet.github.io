💾 NAS Storage Guide

A NAS (Network Attached Storage) is a dedicated storage system connected to your network. It allows multiple devices to access files, backups, media libraries, and applications from one location.

🏠 Home NAS

Typical capacity:
4TB - 40TB

  • Family photo backups
  • Movies and music servers
  • Personal cloud replacement
  • Game backups

⚙️ Advanced NAS

Typical capacity:
40TB - 200TB+

  • Large media libraries
  • Multiple users
  • Small businesses

🏢 Enterprise NAS

Capacity:
Hundreds of TB to Petabytes

  • Companies
  • Research facilities
  • Large archives

RAID Protection Explained

RAID Type Purpose
RAID 0 Combines drives for speed but provides no protection.
RAID 1 Mirrors data on two drives for redundancy.
RAID 5 Balances capacity, speed, and protection.
RAID 6 Protects against multiple drive failures.
