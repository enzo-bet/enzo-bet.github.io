NAS Storage Explained

NAS (Network Attached Storage) is a dedicated storage system connected to a network. It allows computers, phones, and other devices to access files from one central location.

🏠 Home NAS

Capacity:
4TB - 40TB

Used for:
Photos, videos, backups, personal servers

⚙️ Advanced NAS

Capacity:
40TB - 200TB+

Used by:
Power users and small businesses

🏢 Enterprise NAS

Capacity:
Hundreds of TB to Petabytes

Used by:
Companies and data centers

Storage Units Explained

Digital storage increases by thousands at each level. Understanding these units helps compare everything from a phone to a data center.

Unit Approximate Size Example
KB (Kilobyte) 1,000 bytes Small text files
MB (Megabyte) 1,000 KB Images, songs, ebooks
GB (Gigabyte) 1,000 MB Phones, games, movies
TB (Terabyte) 1,000 GB Hard drives and media collections
PB (Petabyte) 1,000 TB Large servers
EB (Exabyte) 1,000 PB Global-scale data systems

1GB

Hundreds of books or thousands of photos.

1TB

Hundreds of HD movies or large game libraries.

1PB

Millions of photos and huge archives.
